## Call-to-Action Strategies

### Primary CTAs (High-Intent)
- "Get Your Free Consultation" - Homepage hero
- "Schedule a Discovery Call" - Services pages
- "Start Your Project" - Portfolio pages
- "Talk to Our Experts" - Contact page

### Secondary CTAs (Medium-Intent)
- "View Our Work" - Homepage
- "Learn More" - Service cards
- "Download Case Study" - Portfolio
- "Read Our Blog" - Content sections

### Micro-CTAs (Low-Intent)
- "Subscribe to Newsletter" - Footer
- "Follow Us on LinkedIn" - Social proof
- "Book a Meeting" - Team pages
- "Get Pricing" - Service details

### CTA Placement Strategy
1. **Above the Fold**: Primary conversion goal
2. **Mid-Content**: Contextual CTAs after value propositions
3. **Before Footer**: Final conversion opportunity
4. **Floating Button**: Mobile accessibility

### CTA Copy Guidelines
- Action-oriented language
- Clear value proposition
- Low-commitment language ("Free", "No-Obligation")
- Specific and benefit-driven
- Create urgency when appropriate

## Content Tone and Voice

### Brand Voice Characteristics
- **Professional**: Expert knowledge without being overly technical
- **Approachable**: Friendly and helpful, not intimidating
- **Confident**: Assured without being arrogant
- **Innovative**: Forward-thinking and cutting-edge
- **Trustworthy**: Reliable and transparent

### Writing Guidelines
- Use active voice
- Keep sentences concise (15-20 words average)
- Use second-person ("you", "your") to address readers directly
- Include specific numbers and statistics when possible
- Break up long content with subheadings and bullet points
- Use storytelling for case studies and testimonials
